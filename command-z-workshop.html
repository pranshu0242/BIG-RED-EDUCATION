The program is led by Dr. Blaine Fisher, Director of Global Education at Big Red Education. The workshop is framed around helping students think critically about technology, communicate ideas effectively, and explore how future tools connect with creativity and leadership.

Why This Lab

Future-facing learning built for the next decade.

Command Z is designed for students who want to understand where technology is going and how to participate in shaping it. Instead of passive lectures, the experience uses discussion, experimentation, and project-based exercises to make new technologies feel tangible.

Students engage with emerging ideas across AI, digital storytelling, strategy, and innovation while also building the creative confidence needed to express original thinking.

Hands-on sessions Team exploration Creative experimentation Future-ready mindset
Who It Is For

Students who are curious, creative, and ready to build ahead of the curve.

The workshop is open to students in Grades 8 through 12 who want exposure to the technologies, ideas, and creative practices shaping the future.

Students interested in emerging technology and AI
Learners who enjoy experimentation and practical projects
Teens exploring future careers, innovation, or entrepreneurship
Young creators who want to tell stories and build digitally
Curriculum

Five modules that connect technology, strategy, and imagination.

01

AI and Beyond

Build a foundation in artificial intelligence, its current applications, and the wider future-tech landscape students should understand now.

02

Future Trendspotting

Learn to identify signals of change and make sense of the trends, industries, and shifts that are redefining tomorrow’s world.

03

Marketing in the Digital Age

Explore how brands communicate today, how digital campaigns work, and how strategy, storytelling, and audiences connect.

04

Creative Technology

Use emerging tools to turn ideas into artifacts, experiments, concepts, and creative outputs with real-world resonance.

05

Digital Storytelling

Develop sharper communication by crafting narratives, pitches, and presentations that make complex ideas engaging and clear.

+

Applied Workshop Format

Every module is delivered through interaction, discussion, creation, and reflection so students leave with usable thinking, not just notes.

Daily Flow

A structured day with room for exploration.

Sessions run from 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM IST, balancing concept-building with hands-on experimentation. The experience is designed to keep students active, engaged, and producing work throughout the day.

9:00 AM

Morning launch

Context-setting, trend decoding, and faculty-led exploration of the day’s topic.

Midday

Lab exercises

Students work through prompts, tools, experiments, and collaborative activities.

Afternoon

Build and share

Project development, presentations, critique, and a daily reflection loop.

Average temperatures in Ajmer during July range from around 25C to 35C. The weather is warm and humid, with occasional to moderate rainfall due to the monsoon season. The Mayo College campus, with its lush greenery and shaded avenues, often feels slightly cooler than the city, especially after rain. Light, breathable clothing is recommended. Pack cotton outfits, a light rain jacket or poncho, and quick-dry layers. For outdoor activities, bring comfortable walking or trekking shoes with good grip, as paths may be wet, along with an umbrella or rain protection for camping and excursions.

Carry a torch, basic medicines you may need, mosquito repellent cream, a pair of sports shoes and floaters, as well as regular slippers. Don’t forget essentials like a hair comb, moisturizer, nail clipper, and clothes suitable for the climate. An outdoor kit comprising a small rucksack and a sleeping bag will be required for the outdoor excursion.

All must adhere to propriety and dress modestly. Girls may wear shorts and skirts that are of a decent length. Sleeveless tops with spaghetti straps and low necklines are not permitted.
